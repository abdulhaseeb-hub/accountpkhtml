A 10 Step Guide for Engaging in Brand Development

MyJobs Editorial Team
Updated June 25, 2022

Brand development is an important component of a business marketing strategy. If a company can attract their audience with a powerful branding of their image, they're more likely to succeed in their particular market and generate profit. Learning about brand development strategies can help you implement them within your marketing and advertising campaigns and decide which strategies are the best choice for you and your team. In this article, we discuss brand development and provide 10 steps for engaging in brand development.

What is brand development?

Brand development is a type of marketing strategy that has a goal of increasing an organization's revenue and growth by increasing consumer recognition and positive perceptions of the organization's image, products and services. The factors that can affect the image of an organization include its culture, organizational behavior, mission and goals. Marketing departments are often in charge of brand development. They use marketing methods, such as marketing campaigns, advertisements and public relations, as these are the most effective way to increase consumer awareness and positivity about a business.

How to engage in brand development

There are many ways to conduct brand development, but marketing teams often use similar strategies in order to maximize the competitive advantage they receive from such efforts. Here is a step-by-step process on how to engage in brand development effectively:

  1. Conduct market research

    Market research is an important first step in brand development, because it allows you to gain knowledge about the desires and motivations of customers, which helps you discover what inspires customers to purchase products or services in your particular industry. Your marketing team can craft campaigns and advertisements more effectively based on information gathered through this process. For example, if a car manufacturer concludes from their market research that consumers are looking for more sustainable vehicles, they can craft their campaigns to match this desire.

    Related: Brand ManagerCover Letter Sample

  2. Discover target audience

    From market research, you can then figure out your target audience, or the types of consumers who are more likely to purchase your products or services. The characteristics that can increase consumer interest in a company, product or service include personality, interests, gender, age and culture. For example, a diaper company is likely to discover that its target audience is new parents.

  3. Define a core message

    A core message, or a tagline, is a phrase that encompasses the entire brand of an organization. Your team may use target audience data to craft a core message, such as a phrase that relates to their target audience's driving motivations or interests. This tagline is usually short and easy to remember, for better results of increasing brand recognition. For example, a sports drink company may craft a core message that relates to physical activity, such as Drink. Sweat. Sleep. Repeat.
